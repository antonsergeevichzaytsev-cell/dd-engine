## §2. Resource & Reserves

### 2.1 Deposit type

**Primary gold:**
- Orogenic (Sukhoi Log, Olimpiada, Kupol)
- Epithermal (Kupol, Chukotka, Indonesia Pacific Rim)
- Porphyry gold (Pebble, Oyu Tolgoi, Kyzyl)
- Carlin-type (Nevada — Newmont, Barrick)
- Volcanogenic massive sulphide gold (rare standalone)
- Placer/alluvial (Russian Far East, Yukon)

**Secondary (tailings reprocessing) — Anton signature:**
- Reprocessing historical tailings from prior CIL, gravity, or flotation
- Typical grade: 0.3-1.5 g/t Au residual
- Sometimes Cu, Ag, base metal credits if from polymetallic original
- **Critical:** original recovery efficiency tells you what's recoverable now
  - Old gravity-only tailings: high residual fine gold, can be 1-2 g/t
  - Old CIL tailings: low residual, usually <0.5 g/t, dissolved Au lost in process water
  - Old flotation tailings (gold-bearing sulphide deposits): residual locked in pyrite/arsenopyrite, requires refractory treatment

**Refractory gold:**
- Sulphide-locked (pyrite, arsenopyrite-hosted)
- Carbonaceous (preg-robbing)
- Tellurium-bearing
- Treatment: POX, BIOX, Albion, roast — flowsheet selection critical

### 2.2 Resource classification

| Item | Standard | Verification |
|------|----------|--------------|
| Au grade distribution | Histogram, top-cut applied? | Nugget effect, smoothing risk |
| Spatial continuity | Variogram disclosed? | High-grade halo, vein continuity |
| Drilling density vs deposit type | Tighter for nuggety | Twin holes? duplicate %? |
| QA/QC | Std/blank/duplicate insertion rates | >5% std, >5% blank, >10% duplicates minimum |

**Russian GKZ → JORC pitfalls for gold:**
- ГКЗ A-grade rarely matches Measured (insufficient sample density typically)
- Coarse gold deposits especially prone to overestimation due to inadequate sampling
- **Если seller represents B/C1 grades as Probable Reserves without re-validation — red flag**

### 2.3 Tailings reprocessing-specific resource

**Key questions [Anton signature methodology]:**
1. **Original deposition history** — when, what flowsheet, what assumed losses?
2. **Vertical variability** — tailings deposit in layers, oldest at bottom; grade varies with original ore feed
3. **Sampling pattern** — how was the tailings dam sampled? Drilling on grid? Auger or sonic core?
4. **Recovery test work** — bench, pilot, hot commissioning data? Is target recovery realistic?
5. **Material handling** — tailings often >20% moisture, sometimes >40%; reprocessing requires dewatering or wet handling

**Tailings reprocessing-specific red flags:**
- Resource model based on <100 drill holes for a >50 Mt deposit
- No vertical variability characterized
- Test work conducted on composite sample (not representative of vertical and lateral variability)
- Recovery assumption >70% on tailings with <0.7 g/t — usually optimistic
- No allowance for cyanide-soluble copper consumption (huge cost for some tailings)

### 2.4 Cut-off grade
- For primary gold: typical 0.4-1.0 g/t open pit, 1.5-3.0 g/t underground
- For tailings reprocessing: 0.3-0.5 g/t (low strip cost, but reagent cost matters)
- Calculation transparency: gold price assumption, recovery, processing cost, mining cost

### 2.5 Mine plan / reprocessing plan
- LOM years
- Annual ounces production trajectory
- Stripping ratio (open pit) / development meters (UG)
- Reprocessing rate vs tailings dam volume

### 3.3 Tailings reprocessing (Anton signature)

**Reclamation methods:**
1. **Hydraulic mining (most common):** monitor washes tailings face, slurry pumped to processing. Limitations: ice/frozen tailings in winter (Russia), water availability.
2. **Mechanical dredging:** for wet tailings dam. Less common.
3. **Dry excavation:** for dry-stacked or surface tailings. Truck-and-shovel, conveyor.

**Operational considerations:**
- Tailings face stability during reclamation (slope failure risk during mining)
- Sequencing — can't undermine perimeter dams
- Cyclic seasonal operation (Russia: 6-9 months effective)
- Water management: process water source, recycle from reprocessing tailings dam to new dam

**Key cost driver:** typical 30-50% of operating cost is reagents (cyanide, lime, activated carbon). Mining cost for tailings is low ($0.5-2/t) compared to primary ore.

### 3.4 Placer (if applicable)
- Resource is unconsolidated gravel
- Mining via dragline, dredge, hydraulic
- Gravity recovery dominant
- Environmental sensitivity (river disturbance)

## §4. Mineral Processing — Gold-Specific Flowsheets

### 4.1 Flowsheet selection logic

| Ore characteristic | Recommended flowsheet | Typical recovery |
|---------------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Free gold, coarse | Gravity + cyanidation tail | 90-96% |
| Free gold, fine | CIL/CIP | 88-94% |
| Sulphide-locked (refractory) | Flotation → POX/BIOX → CIL | 85-92% |
| Carbonaceous (preg-robbing) | Resin-in-leach (RIL) or roast | 80-90% |
| Tellurium-bearing refractory | Roast + CIL | 80-88% |
| Tailings reprocessing | Gravity (if residual coarse) + CIL or just CIL | 60-78% |
| Heap leach (low-grade oxide) | Crushing + agglomeration + heap | 65-80% |

### 4.2 Comminution
- Crushing stages (gyratory / jaw / cone)
- Grinding: SAG/Ball, P80 typically 75-150 μm for CIL
- Energy: 12-22 kWh/t for primary gold ore; lower for tailings reprocessing (already ground)

### 4.3 Gravity recovery
- Centrifugal concentrators (Knelson, Falcon)
- Jigs for coarse gold
- Recovery 30-60% of total gold typically (if coarse component exists)
- Gravity gold smelted on-site to doré, bypasses cyanidation circuit
- **Critical for tailings reprocessing:** check if original processing had gravity — residual coarse gold might be exploitable

### 4.4 CIL / CIP (Carbon-in-Leach / Carbon-in-Pulp)

**Key parameters:**
| Parameter | Industry range | Verification |
|-----------|----------------|--------------|
| Cyanide concentration | 300-1,500 ppm NaCN | Excess for fast assets, lean for cost optimization |
| Dissolved O₂ | 6-10 ppm | Air injection or O₂ |
| pH | 10.5-11.5 | Lime addition |
| Carbon loading | 2,000-15,000 g Au/t carbon | Higher = lower carbon consumption |
| Residence time | 16-48 hours | Lower for tailings re-treat, higher for refractory |
| Cyanide consumption | 0.2-2.5 kg NaCN/t ore | High = preg-robbing, base metals, or sulphides |
| Lime consumption | 0.5-3 kg CaO/t ore | High = sulphide-rich, ARD potential |

### 4.5 Carbon stripping and gold recovery
- Pressure stripping (Zadra) or atmospheric stripping (AARL)
- Electrowinning (cathode sludge)
- Acid wash and reactivation
- Smelting to doré bar (typically 80-90% Au, balance Ag + base metals)

### 4.6 Refractory gold treatment

**POX (Pressure Oxidation):**
- Autoclave 200-225°C, 30-40 bar O₂ partial pressure
- Capex very high ($100-300/t feed capacity), OpEx moderate
- Recovery typically 92-96% from sulphide
- Examples: Olimpiada (Russia), Lihir (PNG), Macraes (NZ), Goldstrike
- Anton-relevant: POX commercial knowledge — used or evaluated for Russian refractory assets

**BIOX (Bacterial Oxidation):**
- Bacteria oxidize sulphides at 30-45°C
- Lower capex than POX, slower kinetics
- Recovery 88-94%
- Used: Fairview SA, Sansu Ghana, Suzdal Kazakhstan

**Albion (atmospheric oxidation):**
- Ultrafine grinding + atmospheric O₂ leach
- Recovery 75-90%
- Used: Las Lagunas Dominican Republic

**Roasting (oldest):**
- Fluidized bed roaster
- Air pollution issues (SO₂, As, Hg)
- Recovery 90-95%
- Examples: Olimpiada (also runs roaster), Twin Creeks (Newmont)

**Killer question:** Какое test work выбора flowsheet refractory treatment, в каких диапазонах какие recoveries? Если flowsheet выбран без bench/pilot/locked-cycle testing → red flag.

### 4.7 Tailings reprocessing specifics [Anton signature]

**Differences from greenfield CIL:**
1. **Already ground** — comminution may be skipped or minimal (regrind only if liberation issue)
2. **Density management** — tailings recovered as slurry, may need water addition or dewatering
3. **Reagent consumption typically higher** — cyanide consumed by partially oxidized sulphides, base metals
4. **Recovery target typically lower** — 60-78% range (vs 85-94% for fresh ore)
5. **Tailings disposal — secondary tailings dam needed**, environmental sensitivity high (already remediated tailings being re-mobilized)
6. **Material movement balance:** mass volume usually equal in/out, but density may differ

**Red flag patterns:**
- Recovery assumption 80%+ on tailings with mixed history without locked-cycle test confirmation
- Reagent assumption based on fresh ore data
- No secondary tailings storage solution
- Permit gaps for re-disturbing original tailings (some jurisdictions require fresh EIA)

### 4.8 Heap leach (oxide gold)
- Heap construction: lined pad, ore crushing typically -25mm
- Agglomeration (cement, lime)
- Solution application: drip irrigation typically
- Solution collection in ponds, ADR (adsorption-desorption-recovery) plant
- Cycle: 60-180 days typically
- Recovery 65-80% typically (lower than CIL but lower capex/opex)

## §6. ESG / HSE / Tailings — Critical for Gold

### 6.1 Cyanide management

**International Cyanide Management Code:**
- Voluntary code; signatory operations comply with audited standards
- Storage, transport, use, monitoring
- Emergency response (cyanide spill, leak)
- Worker exposure controls

**Major incidents to reference:**
- Baia Mare 2000 (Romania): tailings dam failure, 100,000 m³ cyanide-contaminated water into Danube
- Karamken 2009 (Russia, Magadan): tailings dam failure, river contamination
- Various heap leach pad failures historically

**Verification:**
- Cyanide code certification status
- Last audit findings
- Emergency response drill records
- Cyanide consumption reconciliation (purchased vs metallurgically consumed vs destroyed in tails)

### 6.2 Cyanide destruction
**Tails detoxification options:**
1. **SO₂/Air (INCO process):** widely used, effective, $0.5-2/t cost
2. **Hydrogen peroxide:** clean but expensive
3. **Caro's acid:** good for high CN strength
4. **Iron precipitation:** secondary
5. **Natural attenuation in pond:** only if climate permits, requires extended residence

**Discharge standards:**
- WAD CN (weak acid dissociable) typically <0.5 mg/l for surface water
- WAD CN <50 mg/l for tailings impoundment per ICMC
- Free CN < detection in groundwater

### 6.3 Mercury
- If Hg present (some refractory gold assets), retort capture
- Mercury inventory and disposal
- Air emissions Hg
- Worker exposure

### 6.4 Arsenic
- Refractory gold often arsenopyrite-hosted → As liberated during POX/BIOX
- As fixation: ferric arsenate (stable scorodite) precipitation
- Storage of As-bearing residues — designed permanent containment
- Regulatory scrutiny especially Canada, Australia, EU

### 6.5 Tailings Storage Facilities

**Critical for gold:**
- Cyanide-bearing tailings (until destruction)
- ARD potential (sulphide-bearing tailings)
- Heavy metal content
- High volume relative to gold contained

**Same critical assessment as Cu-Ni:**
- Dam construction type (upstream highest risk)
- ITRB / GISTM compliance
- Closure plan funding
- Historical incidents at facility
- See `intel/tailings_intel.md` for sector cross-reference

### 6.6 Water
- Process water source and quantity
- Surface water and groundwater monitoring
- Water treatment (cyanide destruction, metals removal, ARD treatment)
- Dewatering discharge permits

### 6.7 Community and Indigenous
- Especially sensitive for gold (perceived "destructive" mining)
- FPIC (Free, Prior, Informed Consent) status
- Benefit sharing agreements
- Artisanal/illegal mining presence — security risk + community relations risk
- Cyanide perception in community

### 6.8 Closure
- Pit lake water chemistry (if open pit converts to lake)
- Tailings cover (saturated, dry, soil)
- Long-term water treatment requirement
- Reclamation bond vs estimated true cost
- Post-closure monitoring (typically 25+ years)

### 6.9 Carbon footprint
- Scope 1: mining diesel, processing heat
- Scope 2: grid power
- Scope 3: cyanide manufacture, lime, carbon
- Carbon intensity per oz Au: industry range 0.5-2.0 tCO₂/oz; refractory POX/roast higher
- Renewable power transition cases (e.g., solar+battery at remote sites)

## §17. Recommendation Logic

### PROCEED:
- AISC 2nd quartile or better
- Reserve replacement ratio >100% recent
- Cyanide code certified, audit current
- Tailings ITRB compliant, GISTM aligned
- Refining destination current and resilient
- Closure liability funded or quantified

### PROCEED WITH CONDITIONS:
- Price adjustment for [specific finding]
- Escrow for [closure / refining contingency / tailings remediation]
- R&W on [resource model / refractory recovery / permits]
- Post-close 100-day [refining diversification / cyanide audit / community engagement]

### DO NOT PROCEED:
- Active SDN ownership
- Cyanide spill ongoing remediation with regulator
- Tailings dam category 4+ failure consequence without funded remediation
- Resource fraud indicators
- Refining destination completely lost without alternative
- Refractory flowsheet known not to work for ore (e.g., chosen roast for As+Hg+C ore)
