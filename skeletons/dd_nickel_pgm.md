# DD Skeleton — Nickel + PGM Asset

**Применимость:** интегрированный комбинат Cu-Ni sulphide → flotation → smelting → refining → PGM recovery. Также частично применимо к laterite Ni (HPAL) — см. отдельные секции [HPAL]. NPI / matte-only assets — упрощённый scope.

**Anton signature domain:** Норникель (Talnakh, Kola, Polar Division). Глубокое знание Cu-Ni sulphide circuit, PGM recovery, autoclave/Vanyukov technology, dust/SO₂ capture.

**Авторские сильные стороны на этом типе актива:**
- PGM (Pt/Pd/Rh/Ir/Ru) by-product economics — массовый blind spot аналитиков
- Sulphur capture compliance (Серная программа 2.0)
- Talnakh-style massive sulphide vs disseminated ore handling
- Old-Soviet plant brownfield modernisation patterns
- Underground mining at depth + permafrost geomechanics

## §1. Ownership, Sanctions, Counterparty Risk

### 1.1 UBO cascade
- Ownership tree to ultimate UBO. Никаких «институциональных инвесторов» без раскрытия.
- Каждый уровень: jurisdiction, sanctions exposure (OFAC SDN, EU consolidated list, UK OFSI, UN, Swiss SECO).
- For CIS assets post-2022: detailed analysis of any **direct or beneficial** Russian ownership >10%, even if cascaded through Cyprus/UAE/Kazakhstan/Switzerland.

**Critical for Norilsk-style assets:** проверить нет ли OFAC Directive 4 (debt/equity restrictions) даже если actor не SDN.

### 1.2 PGM-specific export licensing
- **Pt/Pd/Rh/Ir/Ru** под dual-use exports control в большинстве jurisdictions (industrial + catalytic + jewellery + medical + military).
- Russian PGM post-2022: **LBMA delisted Russian refiners** (Krastsvetmet, Prioksky). Это влияет на marketability финального продукта.
- China import licences на PGM concentrate vs metal.
- Japan import preferences для Russian PGM.

**Killer question:** Какая доля PGM выручки sold to LBMA-listed buyers vs non-LBMA? Что произошло с этой долей в 2022-2024?

### 1.3 Counterparty concentration
- Top-5 customers by revenue (separately for Ni, Cu, PGM streams).
- Off-take agreements: term, pricing formula, take-or-pay, sanctions clauses.
- Если >40% выручки на одного контрагента — это standalone risk.

## §2. Resource & Reserves

### 2.1 Geology classification

**Sulphide deposits (Norilsk-type, Sudbury-type, Voisey's Bay):**
- Massive sulphide vs disseminated vs stringer/breccia
- Pentlandite [(Fe,Ni)₉S₈] / chalcopyrite / pyrrhotite / pyrite ratio
- PGM hosting: solid solution in pentlandite vs discrete PGM minerals (sperrylite PtAs₂, moncheite (Pt,Pd)(Te,Bi)₂)
- Cu:Ni ratio + variability across ore body

**Laterite deposits [HPAL or atmospheric leach]:**
- Limonite vs saprolite zones
- Fe/Mg ratio (acid consumption driver)
- Co credit grade

### 2.2 JORC / NI 43-101 / GKZ classification

| Item | Standard | Verification |
|------|----------|--------------|
| Measured/Proven | Drilling density per JORC Table 1 | Cross-check assay QA/QC, twin holes, std/blank insertion rate |
| Indicated/Probable | Spacing per deposit type | Geostatistical variogram disclosed? |
| Inferred | Not in mine plan | Mine plan must NOT depend on Inferred |
| Cut-off grade | NiEq or NSR | Calculation transparency — what metal prices, recoveries, payabilities? |

**Russian GKZ A/B/C₁/C₂** conversion to JORC: B/C₁ → Indicated typically; A → Measured rarely (sample density requirements stricter in JORC). **Если seller представляет ГКЗ-классификацию напрямую как JORC — red flag.**

### 2.3 NiEq formula sanity check
- Standard: NiEq = Ni% + Cu%×(Cu_price/Ni_price)×(Cu_rec/Ni_rec)×(Cu_pay/Ni_pay) + PGM_g/t × (PGM_basket_$/Ni_price/22.046) × ...
- **Common manipulation:** assumed Cu recovery 95% in NiEq when actual mill recovery 88%. Boost cut-off illusion.
- **Verify:** разложить NiEq формулу в спецификацию и пересчитать в текущих metal prices.

### 2.4 PGM grade — the silent value lever
- For Norilsk-type: PGM content can be 0.5-15 g/t Pt+Pd combined; rare in disseminated, rich in massive sulphide.
- For Stillwater-type (J-M Reef): Pd:Pt = 3.5:1.
- For Bushveld UG2/Merensky: different mineralogy, requires different flowsheet.
- **PGM recovery typically 75-85% in concentrator + further losses in smelter dust + slag → final 65-75% recovery overall.** Each 5% recovery loss on PGM is huge $/t.

**Killer question:** Какая PGM recovery в концентрате, в шламе, в готовом PGM продукте? Какие losses в шлаке, dust, slag?

### 2.5 Mine plan
- LOM (life of mine) — what reserves, what scenario (price, recovery)
- Stripping ratio (open pit) or backfill % (underground)
- Cut-off grade evolution over LOM
- Ramp-up schedule for any expansion

### 3.2 Underground (Norilsk Talnakh, Kola GMK, Mt Keith, Mt Isa)

**Critical for Norilsk-style at depth (1,500m+):**
- Mining method: sublevel caving, cut-and-fill, room-and-pillar with backfill, sublevel stoping

**Red flag — deferred mining capex pattern:**
- Capital development meters/year falling vs LOM plan
- Development behind production face
- Production from inferred mineralization "next year" promises

## §4. Mineral Processing (Concentrator)

### 4.1 Flowsheet baseline (sulphide)

Crusher → SAG/Ball mill → Flotation (bulk Cu-Ni then differential) → Thickening → Filtration → Concentrate

**Key indicators to verify:**
| Parameter | Industry range | Red flag |
|-----------|----------------|----------|
| Ni feed grade | 0.5-2.5% | Below 0.5%: economics depend entirely on PGM credit |
| Ni recovery to concentrate | 80-92% | <80%: flowsheet issue or refractory ore |
| Cu recovery | 88-95% | <88%: oxidation or pentlandite-chalcopyrite locking |
| PGM recovery to concentrate | 75-85% | <75%: significant gravity losses or discrete PGM losses |
| Ni concentrate grade | 8-18% | <8%: dilution issues, smelter penalties |
| Cu concentrate grade (if separated) | 22-30% | <22%: smelter penalties |
| Mill throughput vs design | ±5% | -15% or more: bottleneck identified? |

### 4.2 Flotation circuit detail

- Roughers / scavengers / cleaners / re-cleaners architecture
- Reagent suite: collectors (xanthates SIBX, PAX, SIPX, dithiophosphates), depressants (CMC, guar, dextrin), pH modifiers (lime), frothers (MIBC, glycols)
- Reagent consumption $/t ore (benchmark: $1.5-4/t for Cu-Ni sulphide)
- Whether differential flotation works (Cu concentrate separate from Ni concentrate) or bulk-only

### 4.3 Comminution circuit

- SAG mill power / ball mill power kWh/t — benchmark to Bond Work Index of ore
- Liner wear pattern, replacement cycle
- Trommel and cyclone classification efficiency
- **Specific energy consumption SAG+Ball typical 15-25 kWh/t for sulphide; >30 kWh/t → check ore hardness change or circuit imbalance**

### 4.4 By-products and deportment

Quantify deportment of each valuable element across products:
- Ni → concentrate / tailings / process water / slag
- Cu → same
- PGM → same (CRITICAL — small losses = big $)
- Co → same
- Au, Ag, Se, Te, Re — often credits
- Penalty elements: As, Sb, Bi, Cd, Pb, Hg, F, Cl

**Bagdad-style audit:** на каждый element входящий в концентрат проверить mass balance closure ±2%. Открытый mass balance >5% = либо измерения врут, либо потери не учтены.

### 4.5 Concentrate logistics
- Транспорт concentrate to smelter (internal / external)
- Moisture content (typical 8-10%)
- Storage capacity (days of production)
- Hazardous classification (sulphide + dust + nickel carcinogen status)

## §5. Smelting

### 5.1 Smelter technology

**Sulphide matte smelting technologies (in order of efficiency):**
1. **Flash smelting (Outotec, INCO):** autogenous, high SO₂ capture, lowest energy. Industry benchmark. Operates at Harjavalta, Sudbury INCO, Jinchuan.
2. **Vanyukov / Top-blown (TBRC):** Russian/Soviet origin. Operates at Norilsk Nadezhda, Mednogorsk. Less efficient than flash but accepts varied feed.
3. **Electric furnace (Stillwater type):** for low-S concentrate or post-roasting.
4. **Reverberatory furnace:** legacy, being phased out (high emissions).
5. **Bath smelting (ISASMELT, Mitsubishi continuous):** modern, gaining share.

**Anton-domain technologies seen on site:**
- Vanyukov at Norilsk Combine (knowledge depth: ✓✓✓)
- Flash smelting (knowledge: ✓✓)
- Electric furnace from PGM perspective (knowledge: ✓✓)

### 5.2 Smelter KPIs

| Parameter | Benchmark | Red flag |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Ni recovery smelting | 95-98% | <95%: slag chemistry issue or feed dilution |
| Cu recovery smelting | 96-99% | <96%: matte-slag separation issue |
| PGM recovery smelting | 95-98% | <95%: PGM-bearing dust losses uncaptured |
| Slag Ni content | <0.3% | >0.5%: significant value loss |
| SO₂ capture rate | >95% (modern), >85% (older) | <85%: massive compliance risk |
| Specific energy consumption smelting | 2.5-5 GJ/t feed | >6 GJ/t: efficiency problem |
| Matte grade (Ni+Cu) | 30-75% | Outside range: flowsheet redesign needed |

### 5.3 SO₂ and dust capture

**Серная программа 2.0 (Norilsk):** $3.5B+ program 2014-2024 for SO₂ capture. If asset has SO₂ capture <90%, либо capex pending, либо compliance issue.

- Acid plant capacity vs SO₂ generated (sulphuric acid byproduct — what market?)
- Gypsum stockpile (alternative to acid)
- Stack emissions vs jurisdictional limits (Russian СанПиН, EU IED, US EPA)
- Dust capture: ESP, baghouse, scrubber — efficiency and OPEX

**Killer question:** Какой % серы in feed captured vs emitted? Какой план достижения 95%+ если ниже?

### 5.4 Refractory lifecycle
- Furnace campaign life (months between major reline)
- Refractory consumption $/t metal
- Reline outage duration and cost

### 5.5 Off-gas heat recovery
- Waste heat boiler — operational? bypassed?
- Steam generation kWh equivalent / t feed
- ORC (organic Rankine cycle) for low-grade heat — increasingly common

## §6. Refining

### 6.1 Base metal refinery (Ni, Cu)

**Ni refinery technologies:**
1. **Pressure leaching (Sherritt-Gordon, Sumitomo MCLE):** hydrometallurgical, autoclave. Norilsk Polar Division uses autoclaves.
2. **Carbonyl process (INCO Mond):** Ni(CO)₄ formation/decomposition. Only INCO Sudbury and Norilsk Kola use this. Very specific safety regime.
3. **Electrorefining (matte electrolysis):** Falconbridge, Kristiansand. Used for high-Ni matte.
4. **Chloride leaching (Falcondo):** less common.

**Cu refinery:** electrorefining from anodes, standard 99.99% cathode quality.

**KPIs:**
- Ni cathode purity (≥99.8% LME-grade, 99.99% premium)
- Co recovery from Ni refinery (typical 80-90%, can be 95% with proper circuit)
- PGM recovery to refinery slimes/residues (CRITICAL — PGM concentrates here before final refining)

### 6.2 PGM refinery

**Если asset includes PGM refinery (Krastsvetmet, Prioksky, Matthey, Heraeus, Anglo PMR):**

- PGM circuit: precipitation, ion exchange, solvent extraction, calcination
- Final products: Pt sponge, Pd sponge, Rh sponge, Ir, Ru — purity 99.9%-99.99%
- LBMA accreditation status (CRITICAL post-2022 for Russian refiners)
- Working capital tied up in PGM circuit (typical 6-12 months due to PGM holding time)
- Customer base for refined PGM (auto catalyst, jewellery, electronics, hydrogen economy)

**Killer question:** Какой средний lock-up time PGM в refinery (от feed до sold product)? При $1,000/oz Pd holding 6 months — это значительный working capital.

### 6.3 By-product recovery
- Co recovery (key credit for laterite Ni)
- Se, Te, Re (from Cu refinery slimes)
- Au, Ag from PGM circuit
- H₂SO₄ from smelter
- Steam from waste heat

## §7. ESG / HSE / Tailings

### 7.1 SO₂ emissions

**Norilsk historical baseline:** ~1.8 Mt SO₂/year peak (2000s), reduced to <0.4 Mt by 2024 via Серная программа.

**Verification:**
- Continuous emission monitoring system (CEMS) data
- Independent stack tests
- Regional air quality monitoring
- Community health metrics in vicinity

### 7.2 Heavy metal soil/water contamination
- Soil Ni, Cu, Co, As content in plant vicinity (multi-decade legacy at old Soviet sites)
- Groundwater monitoring program — bore hole network, parameters, frequency
- Surface water (rivers, lakes) chemistry trends
- Sediment contamination (особенно Norilsk Pyasino lake, Karabash CHEMK area)

**Norilsk-specific:** Pyasino lake contamination, 2020 diesel spill ($2B liability paid). Если asset shares this drainage area — verify clean-up status, ongoing monitoring obligations.

### 7.3 Air quality & community health
- PM2.5/PM10 monitoring stations
- Lichen surveys (bioindicator for SO₂/heavy metals)
- Epidemiological data for nearby populations
- Indigenous communities (Northern peoples in Russian Arctic, First Nations in Canada, Sami in Norway) — Free Prior Informed Consent status

### 7.4 Tailings Storage Facilities (TSF)

**Critical for any sulphide operation:**

- **Acid Rock Drainage (ARD) potential** — sulphide oxidation produces sulphuric acid + metal leaching. Active management for centuries post-closure.
- Dam construction type: upstream (highest risk, Brumadinho 2019), downstream, centerline, dry stack
- Independent Tailings Review Board (ITRB) — exists? recommendations?
- GISTM (Global Industry Standard on Tailings Management) — compliant?
- Closure plan funding (typically 1.5-3% of capital cost set aside, often underfunded)

**Red flags:**
- Upstream-construction dam in active seismic zone
- Freeboard <1m
- Surface water on top of tailings (not desaturated)
- No emergency action plan
- Pond water chemistry showing increasing metal/sulphate concentrations

**Historical incidents to reference:**
- Brumadinho 2019 (Vale, upstream tailings, 270 deaths)
- Mount Polley 2014 (Imperial Metals, dam failure)
- Mariana/Fundão 2015 (Samarco BHP-Vale)
- Norilsk Talnakh tailings — multiple incidents historically

### 7.5 Decommissioning and closure liability
- Reclamation bond amount vs estimated true closure cost
- Underground void stability post-closure
- Water treatment in perpetuity requirement (sulphide ARD)
- PGM refinery decommissioning (specialized hazardous waste)

**Norilsk-style sites:** closure cost often grossly underestimated due to 60+ years of legacy contamination. Cross-check with comparable closures (Sudbury INCO smelter shutdown costs).

### 7.6 Carbon footprint
- Scope 1 emissions (smelter, mine equipment, on-site power)
- Scope 2 (purchased electricity — major if grid carbon-intensive)
- Scope 3 (logistics, suppliers, downstream conversion)
- Carbon intensity per tonne Ni-eq: industry range 5-25 tCO₂/t Ni-eq; HPAL operations can be 50+ tCO₂/t
- CBAM exposure (if EU customers): see `intel/cbam_carbon_intel.md`

## §9. Operating Costs (C1, AISC, C3)

### 9.1 Cost structure

| Cost driver | Typical % of C1 | Verification |
|-------------|------------------|--------------|
| Power | 15-25% | $/MWh × MWh/t Ni |
| Reagents | 8-12% | Major: collectors, depressants, lime |
| Labour | 15-30% (CIS), 20-35% (Western) | Headcount × salary inc. burden |
| Maintenance | 12-18% | Spend/asset value should be 3-5% |
| Royalties | 2-6% | Government, private royalties |
| Logistics | 5-15% | Higher for remote sites |
| Other (insurance, admin, etc.) | 10-15% | |

### 9.2 C1 (cash cost per pound/tonne Ni after by-product credits)

**Industry benchmark Ni C1:**
- 1st quartile: <$3.0/lb Ni (Western Cu-Ni sulphide with PGM credits, e.g., Norilsk historically <$1/lb due to massive PGM credit)
- Median: $4.5-6.0/lb Ni
- 4th quartile: >$8/lb Ni (laterites, low-grade)

**Ni from Norilsk Talnakh:** historically negative C1 (PGM and Cu credits exceed Ni cost). This is the gold standard. If asset cannot get below median, structural cost issue.

### 9.3 AISC (All-in sustaining cost)
- C1 + sustaining capex + corporate G&A
- Should include reclamation accrual, not just operating cash
- Industry typically AISC = C1 × 1.3-1.5

### 9.4 By-product credit calculation
- **Critical:** is PGM credit calculated at LBMA realised price (post-2022 problem for Russian assets) or quoted Pt/Pd/Rh prices?
- Co credit assumption
- Sulphuric acid credit (or cost — if no market, net cost)

**Sanity check:** if asset has 5 g/t PGM in concentrate and reports negative C1, PGM credit is doing 80%+ of the work. Stress-test for PGM price collapse.

### 9.5 Currency exposure
- Revenue: USD-linked (LME)
- Costs: local currency (RUB, CAD, ZAR, AUD) varying %
- FX sensitivity model

## §18. Recommendation Logic

### PROCEED — все из перечисленного:
- Resource adequacy (10+ year LOM at Measured/Indicated)
- Cost position 1st or 2nd quartile
- HSE record acceptable (no fatalities last 3 years, LTIFR trending down)
- Sanctions/UBO clean
- Sustaining capex aligned with depreciation
- Closure liability funded or quantified

### PROCEED WITH CONDITIONS — типовые conditions:
- Price adjustment for [specific finding]
- Escrow $X for [tailings closure / SO₂ compliance / legacy contamination]
- Reps & warranties on [PGM recovery / LBMA accreditation status / power contract]
- Post-close 100-day action plan for [maintenance backlog / safety culture / refining accreditation]

### DO NOT PROCEED — single-issue triggers:
- Active SDN ownership chain
- Imminent regulatory action (closure order, capacity reduction mandate)
- Tailings dam category 4+ failure consequence rating without remediation funding
- Workforce safety culture irreparable in deal horizon
- Resource fraud (assay manipulation, double-counted ore)
